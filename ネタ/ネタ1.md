# Vingroup (ビングループ): Vietnam's Bellwether Conglomerate — Company Profile

## TL;DR
- **Vingroup (HOSE: VIC) is Vietnam's largest private conglomerate and, since November 2025, the first Vietnamese company to exceed VND 1 quadrillion (~$40 billion) in market cap — roughly VND 1,710 trillion (~$60 billion) as of late June 2026 — making it the single largest constituent of the VN-Index and the dominant Vietnamese name in the coming FTSE Russell emerging-market indices.** It posted record 2025 consolidated net revenue of VND 332,770 billion (~$12.8 billion, +76% YoY) and net profit after tax of VND 11,146 billion (+111% YoY, exceeding its AGM plan), driven by a real-estate surge at Vinhomes even as EV arm VinFast continued to burn cash.
- **The group is a barbell of a highly profitable property engine (Vinhomes) and a deeply loss-making EV bet (VinFast, Nasdaq: VFS), bankrolled by founder Phạm Nhật Vượng** — Vietnam's richest man and Southeast Asia's wealthiest (net worth variously estimated at ~$24.5 billion to ~$34.6 billion by Forbes in 2026; see Caveats). VinFast reported a 2025 net loss of VND 99,582.8 billion (~$3,964 million) and carries a formal going-concern warning; Vượng and Vingroup have pledged billions in support and are now restructuring VinFast into an "asset-light" model.
- **For Japanese institutional investors, Vingroup is the key single-stock proxy for Vietnam's September 21, 2026 FTSE upgrade to Secondary Emerging Market status** — Bloomberg notes Vingroup "will likely receive the lion's share of any increase in passive flows, making it an easy proxy bet" — but it comes with elevated leverage, related-party-transaction, and concentration risks that make it a high-beta bet on Vietnam itself.

## Key Findings

**Scale and structure.** Vingroup traces to Technocom, an instant-noodle business (brand "Mivina") that Phạm Nhật Vượng built in Ukraine in the 1990s; he redirected capital to Vietnam from 2001, founding Vinpearl (resorts) and Vincom (real estate), which merged into Vingroup in 2011 (renamed from Vincom JSC to Vingroup JSC in April 2012). Headquartered in Hanoi and listed on HOSE as VIC, the group organizes around five pillars: industrials & technology, real estate & services, infrastructure, green energy, and social enterprises. Founder-and-family control is ~65%, with Vượng personally holding ~10.05% of VIC.

**Financials.** 2025 consolidated net revenue VND 332,770 billion (~$12.8 billion), +76% YoY — the first Vietnamese private firm past VND 300 trillion. Net profit after tax VND 11,146 billion (+111%). Total assets rose to roughly VND 1.12 quadrillion (~$43 billion). Debt is high: VIC's total debt/equity was ~233% (most-recent-quarter, mid-2026). The group has been raising capital aggressively — an offshore bond program (a $325 million Vienna-listed issue in late 2025, plus a planned $350 million Vinpearl-linked issue in 2026), onshore private placements, and a 1-for-1 bonus share issue (3.85 billion new shares) that doubled charter capital to over VND 77 trillion.

**Vinhomes (HOSE: VHM)** is the profit engine: 2025 record contracted sales of VND 205.3 trillion (~$7.9 billion, roughly double 2024), full-year revenue ~VND 153 trillion, net income ~VND 42 trillion (margin ~27%). Market cap ~VND 420–630 trillion depending on date (~$21 billion, June 2026). It declared the largest cash dividend in Vietnamese listed-company history (VND 24,644 billion) in 2026 and is developing mega-projects including the ~$9 billion Vinhomes Green Paradise reclamation city in Cần Giờ, HCMC.

**VinFast (Nasdaq: VFS).** Delivered 196,919 EVs in 2025 (+102% YoY) plus 406,498 e-scooters; revenue ~$3.6 billion (+105%). But 2025 net loss was VND 99,582.8 billion (~$3,964 million), operating cash outflow VND 44,461.2 billion (~$1,770 million), total debt VND 82,164.2 billion (~$3,271 million), and gross margin −42.5% (improved from −57.4% in 2024); auditors flagged substantial going-concern doubt. It opened plants in Tamil Nadu, India (inaugurated August 2025) and Subang, Indonesia (December 2025), while its $4 billion North Carolina plant was repeatedly delayed (now ~2028) and drew a lawsuit from the state. In May 2026 VinFast announced an "asset-light" restructuring selling its Vietnam manufacturing arm (VFTP) for ~$530 million to an investor group tied to Vượng.

**Founder.** Phạm Nhật Vượng, born 1968, is Vingroup chairman and VinFast CEO and Southeast Asia's richest person. He pledged personal grants of up to VND 50 trillion (~$2.1 billion) to VinFast through 2026. He first appeared on the Forbes list in 2013 at $1.5 billion (ranked 974th).

**Strategic significance.** VIC, VHM and VRE together drive an outsized share of the VN-Index — analysts estimate that stripping out the Vingroup complex would have left the VN-Index near 1,400 at end-2025 instead of ~1,785, i.e., "one conglomerate accounting for nearly 400 index points." Vingroup is the largest single constituent in FTSE's Vietnam index and is expected to receive the largest passive inflows of any Vietnamese stock upon the September 2026 FTSE upgrade.

## Details

### 1. Company overview and history
Phạm Nhật Vượng studied at the Moscow Geological Prospecting Institute, then founded Technocom in Ukraine in the early 1990s, building the "Mivina" instant-noodle brand into a business with revenue over $100 million before selling it to Nestlé in 2010. He began investing in Vietnam in 2001, founding Vinpearl (hospitality) and Vincom (real estate); the two merged to form Vingroup in 2011, with the parent renamed from Vincom JSC to Vingroup JSC in April 2012. The company is headquartered in Hanoi and listed on HOSE under ticker VIC. As of late June 2026 VIC traded around VND 223,000–228,000, giving a market cap of roughly VND 1,710 trillion. VIC hit an all-time high of VND 242,000 on June 24, 2026.

### 2. Business segments / group structure
- **Vinhomes (HOSE: VHM):** Vietnam's largest developer, majority-owned by Vingroup. Record 2025 contracted sales VND 205.3 trillion; FY2025 revenue ~VND 153 trillion, net income ~VND 42 trillion (margin ~27%). Mega-projects: Vinhomes Green Paradise (Cần Giờ, 2,870 ha, ~VND 217 trillion/~$9 billion), Vinhomes Wonder City (Hanoi), Vinhomes Royal Island (Hai Phong), Ocean City, plus Ha Long Xanh, Cam Ranh Bay, and the Olympic Sports Urban Area in Hanoi (~$35 billion, launched Dec 2025).
- **VinFast (Nasdaq: VFS):** Founded 2017, went public via SPAC merger (Black Spade) August 2023, briefly reaching ~$82–85 billion market cap before collapsing; ~$7 billion market cap by mid-2026 (~$3.10/share, well below its post-SPAC peak). Four manufacturing facilities with combined capacity ~600,000 EVs/year. India (Tamil Nadu, 50k→150k capacity) and Indonesia (Subang, 50k) plants operational; North Carolina delayed to ~2028. V-Green (charging, 90% owned by Vượng) spun off 2024. Green SM (GSM) electric taxi operates in Vietnam and Indonesia.
- **Vinpearl (HOSE: VPL):** Hospitality/tourism arm; re-listed on HOSE May 13, 2025 at VND 71,300/share (~1.8 billion shares, ~VND 130 trillion valuation, top-15 by market cap on HOSE). 2024 revenue ~VND 14.4 trillion, net profit VND 2.55 trillion. ~31 hotels/resorts (16,100+ rooms), VinWonders parks, golf courses.
- **Vincom Retail (HOSE: VRE):** Retail-mall operator; Vingroup divested control (via SDI/Sado) in early 2024.
- **Social enterprises:** VinMec (healthcare, not-for-profit; now expanding into surgical robotics), Vinschool (education), VinUniversity, VinBus (electric buses).
- **Technology:** VinAI (sold generative-AI unit MovianAI to Qualcomm, April 2025; VinBrain sold to Nvidia late 2024), VinRobotics, VinBigData, VinMotion. New units: VinMetal (steel, ~VND 10 trillion, 5 Mt Phase 1 at Vung Ang), VinSpeed (high-speed rail), VinEnergo, Vin New Horizon (retirement/wellness communities).
- **Divestitures/exits:** VinCommerce (VinMart/VinMart+) and VinEco sold to Masan (Dec 2019); Adayroi (e-commerce) and VinPro (electronics retail) shut down (2019); VinSmart phones/TVs discontinued (2021); Vinpearl Air aborted; withdrew from the ~$67 billion North-South high-speed rail bid (Dec 2025). This pattern of decisive "close-fast, cut-clean" exits to concentrate resources is a hallmark of Vượng's strategy.

### 3. Founder profile — Phạm Nhật Vượng
Chairman of Vingroup and CEO of VinFast. Controls ~50% of Vingroup directly and ~65% with family (~10.05% personal VIC stake), and ~97–98% of VinFast via private vehicles. Personally pledged up to VND 50 trillion (~$2.1 billion) in grants to VinFast through 2026. His wife Phạm Thu Huong (Vingroup vice-chair) and sister-in-law Phạm Thúy Hằng are also billionaires. In May 2026 his son Phạm Nhật Quân Anh was named VinFast board chairman. Net-worth estimates in 2026 range widely and move with VIC's share price (see Caveats).

### 4. Financial performance and VinFast support
Vingroup 2025 consolidated revenue VND 332,770 billion (+76%), net profit after tax VND 11,146 billion (+111%). For 2026 the group targets net revenue ~$17.7 billion (+35.6%) and net profit ~$984 million (+125.9%). VinFast remains the key drag: 2025 net loss VND 99,582.8 billion (~$3,964 million) on the VFS Ltd. basis (a Singapore SFRS group basis showed a larger VND ~120 trillion net loss), operating cash outflow ~$1.77 billion, total debt ~$3.27 billion, going-concern warning. VinFast closed 2025 with ~$3.1 billion total liquidity, including ~$1.1 billion from the founder and a ~$413 million Vingroup borrowing commitment.

Support mechanisms (announced December 2024): Vingroup to lend up to VND 35 trillion ($1.4 billion) through 2026; Vượng to grant up to VND 50 trillion ($2.1 billion); conversion of ~VND 80 trillion ($3.3 billion) of loans into dividend-entitled preferred shares. The May 2026 asset-light restructuring transfers ~VND 182 trillion (~$6.9 billion) of debt to the sold manufacturing entity VFTP, leaving VinFast Auto Ltd. effectively debt-free; VinFast targets EBITDA profitability in 2027.

Capital raises 2025–2026: $325 million offshore bonds (Vienna, late 2025, Vinpearl-linked); planned $350 million offshore bond (2026, Vinpearl equity option); VND 1 trillion onshore private placement for debt restructuring; guarantees of VinFast bonds; a 1-for-1 bonus issue doubling charter capital to over VND 77 trillion.

### 5. Stock performance
VIC surged roughly fivefold in 2025 and continued to new highs in 2026 (ATH VND 242,000, June 24, 2026), up ~371% year-on-year by mid-2026. Vingroup became the first Vietnamese company to exceed VND 1 quadrillion market cap in November 2025. VHM rose sharply too (up ~148% in the year to March 2026), hitting ceiling prices around dividend/earnings catalysts (e.g., VND 161,500 on May 7, 2026 after a record dividend and Q1 results covering 42.7% of the full-year profit target). Both VIC and VHM are in the VN30; real estate/construction (incl. VIC, VHM, VRE) is ~22.8% of VN30. VinFast (VFS) traded ~$3.10 with ~$7 billion market cap by mid-2026.

**FTSE Russell upgrade.** FTSE confirmed (Oct 7, 2025; reaffirmed at the March/April 2026 interim review) Vietnam's reclassification from Frontier to Secondary Emerging Market, effective September 21, 2026, phased in four tranches through September 2027 (10% Sept 2026, 20% March 2027, 35% June 2027, 35% Sept 2027). Vietnam's projected weight is ~0.35% of FTSE Emerging All Cap and ~0.19–0.23% of the FTSE Emerging Index (data-vintage dependent; SSI's March-2026 vintage revised these slightly lower). VIC is the largest expected Vietnamese constituent; SSI Research estimates ~$498 million of passive inflows to VIC and ~$99 million to VHM. Total FTSE-tracking passive inflow estimates vary: SSI ~$1.4 billion (with a separate SSI Securities figure of ~$1.67 billion spread over three-to-five quarters, likened to Saudi Arabia's 2019 upgrade); FTSE Russell ~$1.5 billion; World Bank ~$5 billion short-term (up to $25 billion by 2030); Vietcap $6–10 billion. Final constituent list is due August 21, 2026.

### 6. Recent news (2025–2026)
- Vinpearl HOSE listing (May 13, 2025).
- VinFast India plant inaugurated (Aug 4, 2025); Indonesia plant opened (Dec 2025); North Carolina delayed to ~2028 and sued by the state (May 2025, seeking recovery of $80.2 million in state funds).
- Vinhomes Green Paradise (Cần Giờ) groundbreaking (April 19, 2025); simultaneous launch of 11 major projects (Dec 19, 2025) incl. Olympic Sports Urban Area, Ha Long Xanh, Cam Ranh Bay, Ben Thanh–Can Gio railway, VinMetal.
- Qualcomm acquired VinAI's MovianAI (April 2025); Nvidia bought VinBrain (late 2024).
- Qatar's JTA Investment MoU exploring $1 billion+ into VinFast.
- VinFast asset-light restructuring / $530 million manufacturing sale led by Berjaya Vietnam CEO Nguyen Hoai Nam (May 2026).
- Withdrawal from North-South high-speed rail bid (Dec 2025).
- Risks flagged in the press: high leverage, related-party transactions (a material share of VinFast deliveries went to affiliated parties — ~19% of Q4 2024 EV deliveries; reports of ~27% of sales to affiliates), VinFast losses/going-concern, and heavy reliance on founder support. Nikkei has repeatedly reported that Vingroup is "selling off main businesses" to fund VinFast, "raising concerns about the group's future cash flow."

### 7. Strategic significance and Japan angle
Vingroup is Vietnam's largest private employer and its largest private taxpayer, paying more than VND 56.2 trillion into the state budget in 2024 (up 82% YoY, ~23% of the Top-100 private firms' total and nearly 40% of the Top-10). The Vingroup complex drives an outsized share of the VN-Index and is the primary vehicle for foreign investors seeking Vietnam exposure and the top expected beneficiary of the FTSE upgrade — Bloomberg calls it "an easy proxy bet."

**Japan–Vietnam angle:** Mitsubishi Corporation signed a broad five-area MoU with Vingroup (May 2024) covering urban development (large-scale investment intent into Vinhomes smart-city projects), data centres, renewable energy, used-cooking-oil recycling, and VinFast international after-sales service. Sumitomo signed an MoU with GSM (green mobility). However, no Japanese institution holds a direct listed-equity stake in VIC/VHM/VFS; the deepest Japanese engagement with a listed Vietnamese firm is with FPT (Sumitomo and SBI each took 20% of FPT Smart Cloud Japan, April 2025). Sumitomo's competing $4.2 billion BRG North Hanoi Smart City project (construction began Aug 2025) reflects Japanese appetite for Vietnamese urban real estate. JICA has worked with Vietnam's State Securities Commission on the market-upgrade capacity-building agenda. Nikkei Asia covers Vingroup/VinFast extensively.

## Recommendations
- **For equity exposure to Vietnam's FTSE upgrade, VIC is the highest-conviction single-name beneficiary but the lowest-quality on fundamentals.** For cleaner exposure to the profitable property engine, prefer VHM (P/E ~10x, D/E ~59%, ~27% margin) over VIC (P/E ~160x, D/E ~233%), whose earnings are diluted by VinFast losses. Holding both captures the "index proxy" flow into VIC while anchoring to VHM's cash generation.
- **Stage entry around the FTSE phasing calendar** (10% Sept 2026, 20% March 2027, 35% June 2027, 35% Sept 2027) and the August 21, 2026 final-constituent announcement. Front-loading risk and post-upgrade profit-taking are real (HSBC has cautioned that anticipation may limit further short-term upside), so avoid chasing pre-upgrade spikes.
- **Treat VinFast (VFS) as a separate, speculative bet**, not a proxy for Vingroup. Watch quarterly gross margin trajectory (−42.5% FY2025, −40% Q4), completion of the $530 million asset-light restructuring (targeted Q3 2026), and progress toward the stated 2026 breakeven / 2027 EBITDA-profit goal and the 300,000-EV 2026 delivery target.
- **Benchmarks that would change the thesis:** VinFast reaching positive gross margin or closing the mooted $1 billion+ external investment (e.g., JTA/Qatar) would de-risk VIC's consolidated profile; conversely, any weakening of founder/Vingroup support letters, a Vinhomes sales slowdown at Cần Giờ/Ocean City, VND depreciation beyond the SBV band, or FTSE-flow disappointment would pressure the stock.

## Caveats
- **Founder net-worth conflict:** Forbes real-time figures for Phạm Nhật Vượng in 2026 span a wide range and track VIC's volatile share price — the Forbes profile showed ~$34.6 billion as of June 25, 2026 and Wikipedia cited ~$34.1 billion (April 2026), while VOV/VnExpress cited ~$24.5 billion (ranked 100th globally, April 5, 2026) and ~$29.2 billion (Jan 2026). Bloomberg's index (which excludes his Vingroup-held VinFast shares to avoid double-counting) uses a different methodology. Use the ~$30+ billion range with a "Forbes, mid-2026" qualifier and note the fluctuation.
- **Data-vintage and source conflicts:** Market-cap and P/E figures vary by source and date (VIC market cap cited from VND 1,657–1,728 trillion across providers; VHM from VND 410–663 trillion around its large dividend/ex-rights adjustments). VinFast's 2025 net loss is ~$3,964 million (VFS Ltd. Form 20-F basis) vs. VND ~120 trillion on the Singapore SFRS group basis — different reporting entities/standards.
- **Forward-looking items are projections, not results:** 2026 targets, FTSE inflow estimates (all analyst forecasts, not realized flows), VinFast breakeven guidance, and the Cần Giờ project economics are management or analyst forecasts.
- **Governance/related-party risk:** A significant share of VinFast deliveries and its restructuring buyers are related to founder Phạm Nhật Vượng; the asset-light sale was priced (~$530 million) well above an independent Grant Thornton DCF midpoint (~$106 million), warranting scrutiny.
- **The Mitsubishi–Vinhomes tie is an MoU (stated intent), not a closed transaction; no committed dollar figure has been disclosed.** No Japanese institution holds a direct listed-equity stake in VIC/VHM/VFS.
